## Chapter 2
### Too much Information
"According to the New York Times, the average person’s daily consumption of information now adds up to a remarkable 34 gigabytes." [[Building a Second Brain.pdf#page=17&selection=18,0,22,53&color=yellow|Building a Second Brain, p.17]] "Instead of empowering us, this deluge of information often overwhelms us. Information Overload has become Information Exhaustion, taxing our mental resources and leaving us constantly anxious that we’re forgetting something." [[Building a Second Brain.pdf#page=17&selection=34,0,36,76&color=yellow|Building a Second Brain, p.17]] 
### Struggle to find information
1. "Research from Microsoft shows that the average US employee spends 76 hours per year looking for misplaced notes, items, or files." [[Building a Second Brain.pdf#page=17&selection=42,0,44,1&color=yellow|Building a Second Brain, p.17]]
2. "International Data Corporation found that 26 percent of a typical knowledge worker’s day is spent looking for and consolidating information spread across a variety of systems" [[Building a Second Brain.pdf#page=17&selection=47,0,48,80&color=yellow|Building a Second Brain, p.17]]
"In other words, we go to work five days per week, but spend more than one of those days on average just looking for the information we need to do our work." [[Building a Second Brain.pdf#page=18&selection=5,0,6,78&color=yellow|Building a Second Brain, p.18]] "Every bit of energy we spend straining to recall things is energy not spent doing the thinking that only humans can do:" [[Building a Second Brain.pdf#page=18&selection=14,0,15,37&color=yellow|Building a Second Brain, p.18]]
### What is a "Second Brain"?
"This digital commonplace book is what I call a **Second Brain**. Think of it as the combination of a study notebook, a personal journal, and a sketchbook for new ideas. It is a multipurpose tool that can adapt to your changing needs over time." [[Building a Second Brain.pdf#page=20&selection=27,0,29,81&color=yellow|Building a Second Brain, p.20]]
