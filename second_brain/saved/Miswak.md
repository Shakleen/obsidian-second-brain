Siwak means cleaning the mouth and teeth with a siwak which is the name given to the tool used. 
## How
* **Tool**: A brush like cleaning tool, be it the bark of an arak tree (which Prophet (SA) used) or a modern brush.
* **Hands**: Right or left. Doesn't matter.
* **Toothpaste**: Encouraged as it cleans the mouth better. <span style="color: red;"> But avoid using strong scented ones in Ramadan. </span>
* **Privately**: It's impolite to do it in front of pubilc.
* Wash siwak tool after use.
* Place it in a clean place.
## When
1. Making [[2025-10-28_How-to-perform-Wudu-or-Ablution|Wudu]] and at times of [[2025-10-28_Salat|Salat]].
   > the Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) said: Were it not for the fact that I did not want to make things too hard for my ummah, I would have commanded them to use the siwak at every time of prayer. **(Reported by al-Bukhari, 2/299 and Muslim, 1/151)**
2. Entering home to be with family.

3. Getting up from sleep.
   > Hudhayfah ibn al-Yaman (may Allah be pleased with him) reported that when the Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) got up during the night, he would clean his mouth thoroughly with the siwak. **(Reported by al-Bukhari, 1/98 and Muslim, 1/220)**
4. The smell of ones mouth changes due to food or empty stomach.
5. When going to the mosque.
