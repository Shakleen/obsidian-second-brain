# Islamic Terminologies

## Level of Obligations

| **Term**                         | **Meaning & Obligation Level**                                                                        | **Consequence of Omission (General View)**                      | **Consequence of Performance**               | **Example Context**                                                                      |     |
| -------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --- |
| **[[Fard]]**                     | **Absolutely Obligatory.** A foundational pillar or commandment from Allah.                           | **[[Major Sin]]** Intentionally omitting it is a grave matter.     | Great reward.                                | The 5 daily obligatory prayers (Salah).                                                  |     |
| **[[Wajib]]**                    | **Required/Necessary.** Very close to _Fard_ but sometimes differentiated by school of thought.       | **Sinful.** Omission is generally treated seriously.            | Reward.                                      | _Witr_ prayer (in the Hanafi school).                                                    |     |
| **[[Sunnat-e-Muakkadah]]**       | **Emphasized Tradition.** Acts the Prophet ($\text{PBUH}$) performed persistently and consistently.   | **Sinful/Highly Disliked** if consistently left without excuse. | Significant reward; completes _Fard_ duties. | The _Sunnah_ prayers accompanying the 5 daily prayers (e.g., 2 _rak'ahs_ before _Fajr_). |     |
| **[[Sunnat-e-Ghair-Muakkadah]]** | **Non-Emphasized Tradition.** Acts the Prophet ($\text{PBUH}$) practiced occasionally but not always. | **No Sin.** Omission is generally overlooked.                   | Reward.                                      | Certain _Sunnah_ prayers associated with _Dhuhr_ or _Asr_.                               |     |
| **[[Nafl]]**                     | **Voluntary/Supererogatory.** Extra acts done purely out of devotion.                                 | **No Sin.** It is optional.                                     | Extra reward; draws one closer to Allah.     | _Tahajjud_ (night prayer) or _Duha_ (mid-morning) prayer.                                |     |

## Spectrum of Halal and Haram
In common usage, Halal often encompasses everything from [[Mustahab]] to [[Mubah]], while [[Haram]] is the clear prohibition, with [[Makruh]] being the area of caution in between.

| **Rank** | **Term**                                                                          | **English Meaning**    | **Consequence if Performed** | **Consequence if Omitted** | **Relation to Halal/Haram**            |
| -------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------- | ---------------------------- | -------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| **1**    | **[[Fard]] / [[Wajib]]**                                                          | Obligatory             | Reward                       | **[[Major Sin]]**          | Outside the scope of general Halal     |
| **2**    | **[[Mustahab]]** ([[Sunnat-e-Muakkadah]], [[Sunnat-e-Ghair-Muakkadah]], [[Nafl]]) | Recommended            | Reward                       | None                       | **Halal** (Encouraged)                 |
| **3**    | **Mubah**                                                                         | Permissible / Neutral  | None                         | None                       | **Halal** (Permitted)                  |
| **4**    | **[[Makruh]]**                                                                    | Disliked / Discouraged | None (unless persistent)     | Minor Reward               | **Generally Halal** (But best avoided) |
| **5**    | **Haram**                                                                         | Forbidden / Prohibited | **[[Major Sin]]**            | Reward                     | Outside the scope of general Halal     |
