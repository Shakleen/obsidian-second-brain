## Part 2: Skincare Integration with Wudu

The frequent washing required for Wudu (face, arms, feet) is a primary cause of dryness for you. The goal is to minimize water-loss and immediately restore the skin barrier.

### Best Practices for Wudu and Dry Skin

1. **Use Lukewarm/Cool Water:** Avoid hot water during ablution.
2. **Pat Dry:** After Wudu, gently **pat** the washed areas (face, arms, feet) with a clean, soft towel instead of rubbing. Leave the skin *slightly* damp.
3. **Moisturize Immediately:** Reapply a thin layer of moisturizer to the washed areas immediately after patting dry, if your skin feels tight or dry.

### Product Recommendations for Very Dry Skin

- **Body Moisturizer:** Ditch light lotions for **thick creams, balms, or body butters** that contain petrolatum, ceramides, or shea butter. Look for fragrance-free options if your skin is also sensitive.
- **Face Cream:** Use a heavier, **occlusive** face cream in the morning that is designed to strengthen the skin barrier (contains ceramides, hyaluronic acid, or petrolatum).
- **For Wudu:** Keep a small tube of your body or face cream near your Wudu area. Applying a thin layer to your face and arms immediately after Wudu will prevent "Wudu-pattern xerosis" (dry skin).
