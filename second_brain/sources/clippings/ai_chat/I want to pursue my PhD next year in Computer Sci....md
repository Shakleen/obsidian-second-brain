### 1\. Robust and Ethical Generative AI for Personalized Educational Content

This topic directly builds on your **Data Science/NLP Masters**, your **Kaggle experience (essay rating/misconception recommendation)**, and addresses the current major trend of **Large Language Models (LLMs)** in education.

- **Focus Area:** Developing LLM-based systems for **Intelligent Tutoring** and **Personalized Learning Paths**.
- **Core Research Questions:**
	- **Generation & Personalization:** How can we fine-tune LLMs (or develop smaller, specialized models) to generate high-quality, pedagogically sound, and context-aware educational content (e.g., *quizzes, explanations, practice problems*) that adapt in real-time to a student's **specific knowledge gaps** and **learning style** (which connects to your misconception work)?
	- **Ethics & Trust (PII & Bias):** Given your PII detection experience, a crucial contribution would be to research **privacy-preserving NLP models** (e.g., using differential privacy or federated learning) for educational data, and methods for **bias detection and mitigation** in generated content, ensuring fairness for all students.
	- **LLM Evaluation:** Creating new **pedagogically-grounded metrics** for evaluating the *quality, correctness, and educational value* of LLM-generated teaching content, going beyond simple perplexity or ROUGE scores.
- **Why you are a fit:** Your experience with PII and essay scoring demonstrates an existing concern for data ethics and qualitative assessment, which is critical in the LLM-in-Education space.

### 2\. Multimodal AI for Analyzing and Enhancing Complex Learning Environments

This topic allows you to integrate your **Computer Vision work experience** with your **NLP/AIEd interest**, putting you at the cutting edge of multimodal research.

- **Focus Area:** Building systems that analyze both **textual (NLP)** and **visual/behavioral (Computer Vision)** data from the learning process to provide richer insights.
- **Core Research Questions:**
	- **Student Engagement & Affective State:** How can you use computer vision (e.g., via webcam) to analyze a student's **gaze, facial expressions, and body language** (attention, confusion, frustration) while they interact with learning content, and combine this with NLP analysis of their **textual input/dialogue** (e.g., in a chat-based tutor)?
	- **Feedback Integration:** Developing an AI system that uses these multimodal signals to provide **real-time, non-intrusive feedback** (e.g., "Take a break, you seem frustrated" or "Let's review the last concept you stared at for a long time").
	- **Annotation Efficiency:** Researching methods to use LLMs to auto-annotate video data (e.g., transcripts, actions) to create better, more scalable training datasets for the vision models.
- **Why you are a fit:** You have the rare combination of **Computer Vision** and **NLP** experience. AIEd is moving beyond pure text, and you can lead this charge by analyzing *how* students learn, not just *what* they write.

### 3\. Knowledge Graph-Enhanced NLP for Adaptive Misconception Diagnosis

This topic is a deeper dive into one of your specific Kaggle experiences (recommending misconceptions), applying advanced techniques to create a structural, explainable model.

- **Focus Area:** Moving from simple classification (e.g., essay score) to **explainable and actionable diagnosis** of student understanding using structured knowledge representations.
- **Core Research Questions:**
	- **Knowledge Representation:** How can you use **Knowledge Graphs (KGs)** to formally represent the **curriculum structure**, common **student misconceptions**, and the **prerequisite relationships** between concepts?
	- **Diagnosis with KGs:** How can NLP models (e.g., Relation Extraction, Text-to-Graph mapping) analyze student work (essays, code, math answers) to link their errors or non-canonical statements directly to a node (misconception) in the Knowledge Graph?
	- **Adaptive Recommendation:** Using the KG structure to power a **recommendation engine** that suggests the *optimal intervention* (e.g., a specific video, a remedial problem) based on the diagnosed misconception and its prerequisites in the graph.
- **Why you are a fit:** Your prior experience with recommending misconceptions shows a clear, practical interest in diagnosing learning difficulties. This topic allows you to apply modern NLP to a fundamental challenge in educational measurement and build a system that is both **effective and inherently explainable**.
